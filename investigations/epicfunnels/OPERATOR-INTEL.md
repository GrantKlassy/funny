## The Corporate Ancestor: Fluent, Inc.

Moxxi Digital is not an independent invention. It is a direct continuation of Fluent, Inc.'s business model, built by Fluent's own people.

### What Fluent Does

Fluent, Inc. (NASDAQ: FLNT) is a publicly traded performance marketing and data company headquartered at 300 Vesey St, 9th Floor, New York, NY 10282. Founded in 2010 by **Ryan Schulke** (CEO) and **Matthew Conlin** (President), who met at St. John's University and previously worked together at Clash Media, a digital advertising network.

Fluent's owned media properties include **Flash Rewards**, **The Smart Wallet** (created by Kevin Riehl), and **FindDreamJobs**. ### The Legal Environment Got Easier

In **January 2025**, the 11th Circuit Court of Appeals **vacated the FCC's one-to-one consent rule** (which would have required consumers to consent to each specific company that would contact them). The FCC formally **eliminated** this requirement in August 2025. This means blanket consent to unnamed "Marketing Partners" — exactly what MyDailySurge's terms require — is currently legal. TrustedForm certificates are valid for **5 years**, meaning a lead collected today can justify calls until 2031. The regulatory environment has become MORE permissive since Moxxi Digital was founded.

This is a separate monetization channel from the CPA lead selling — direct subscription fraud.

### Why Sweepstakes?

The sweepstakes are not the product. The sweepstakes are the **lure**. Nobody at Moxxi Digital cares whether anyone wins a $1,000 prize (administered by SCA Promotions — one drawing per year). The sweepstakes exist because:

1. They attract people willing to give personal information
2. The "entry" process collects name, email, phone, address
3. The TCPA consent checkbox grants permission to sell the data
4. ActiveProspect/TrustedForm "certifies" the consent as valid
5. Jornaya/LeadiD tracks the consumer session for compliance proof
6. The data is now a "TCPA-consented lead" that can be legally sold

SCA Promotions provides sweepstakes legal cover (one real prize/year, Texas law, binding arbitration). Jeffrey Kauffman's compliance expertise makes the paperwork defensible. The entire legal stack exists to make illegitimate data harvesting look like a legitimate promotion.
